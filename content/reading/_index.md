This is my reading list, categorized from most recent to oldest and by year when
possible. For links, I default to author website, publisher website, and/or
independent booksellers - whichever comes up first in a search.

# Current

[Jonny Appleseed](https://arsenalpulp.com/Books/J/Jonny-Appleseed) by Joshua Whitehead

# 2026

- [The Mercy of Gods](https://www.jamessacorey.com/books/the-mercy-of-gods/), James S. A. Corey
- [The Foucault Reader](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.ca/books/55035/the-foucault-reader-by-michel-foucault/9780394713403), Michel Foucault

  - Foucault (along with Fanon) seems to be a major influence on many of my
    favourite socio-political authors. His thoughts around biopower and
    panopticism are very important for analyzing the contemporary world through
    a critical lens.

- [Madness and Civilization: A History of Insanity in the Age of Reason](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.ca/books/55031/madness-and-civilization-by-michel-foucault/9780679721109), Michel Foucault
- [Mutual Aid: Building Solidarity During This Crisis (and the Next)](https://www.versobooks.com/en-ca/products/2722-mutual-aid), Dean Spade
- [Kill Anything That Moves: The Real American War in Vietnam](https://us.macmillan.com/books/9781250045065/killanythingthatmoves/), Nick Turse
- [Embers: One Ojibway's Meditations](https://douglas-mcintyre.com/products/9781771621335), Richard Wagamese
- [Dark Matter](https://michellepaver.com/dark-matter/), Michelle Paver
- [Horrorstör](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.ca/books/211872/horrorstor-by-grady-hendrix/9781594745263), Grady Hendrix
  
  - This book was a very underwhelming start to the year. I should've seen the
    gimmick-y read coming.

# 2025

50 books in all this year - this could be a lifetime peak. I owe it to having an
infant and travelling.

- [The Stone Sky](https://store.orbit-books.co.uk/products/the-stone-sky), N. K. Jemisin
  
  - This entire trilogy was great. Jemisin is a new favourite author of mine. 

- [The Obelisk Gate](https://store.orbit-books.co.uk/products/the-obelisk-gate), N. K. Jemisin
- [Research is Ceremony: Indigenous Research Methods](https://fernwoodpublishing.ca/book/research-is-ceremony-shawn-wilson), Shawn Wilson
  
  - Ontology, Epistemology, Methodology, Axiology. Indigenous ways of knowing
    and being. Circular time. Relationships and accountability applied to a
    respectful research paradigm.

- [Orientalism](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.ca/books/159783/orientalism-by-edward-w-said/9780394740676), Edward Said
- [Mapping the Interior](https://torpublishinggroup.com/mapping-the-interior/), Stephen Graham Jones
- [The Fifth Season](https://www.hachettebookgroup.com/titles/n-k-jemisin/the-fifth-season/9780316229302/), N. K. Jemisin
- [Taaqtumi](https://inhabitbooks.com/products/taaqtumi), Various
- [Bad Indians Book Club](https://www.broadleafbooks.com/store/product/9781506499123/Bad-Indians-Book-Club), Patty Krawec
  
  - Very cool follow-up to *Becoming Kin*, focused on writing from
    marginalization. Particularly interesting look at the Indigenous perspective
    on the apocalyptic and horror genres.

- [The Dawn of Everything](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.ca/books/616885/the-dawn-of-everything-by-david-graeber-and-david-wengrow/9780771049842), David Graeber, David Wengrow

  - Second time reading. This was an eye-opener for me the first time I read it.
    I think I may actually like Graeber's *Debt: The First 5,000 Years* better.

- [The Kaiju Film](https://mcfarlandbooks.com/product/the-kaiju-film/), Jason Barr
- [Book and Dagger](https://www.harpercollins.com/products/book-and-dagger-elyse-graham), Elyse Graham
- [The Old Man and the Sea](https://www.simonandschuster.ca/books/Old-Man-and-the-Sea/Ernest-Hemingway/9780684801223), Ernest Hemingway
- [Pollution is Colonialism](https://www.dukeupress.edu/pollution-is-colonialism), Max Liboiron

  - Lots of insights about ideas of waste, historical views on the "safe" amount
    of pollution which an environment can handle, and so on. Seems important for
    those taking a serious look at changing ecological systems and human
    relationships with them.

- [The Song of Achilles](https://madelinemiller.com/the-song-of-achilles/), Madeline Miller
- [The Prince](https://www.simonandschuster.ca/books/The-Prince/Niccolo-Machiavelli/9781627939461), Niccolo Machiavelli
- [Gravity's Rainbow](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.ca/books/299800/gravitys-rainbow-classics-deluxe-edition-by-thomas-pynchon/9780698151260), Thomas Pynchon

  - What a monster of a book.

- [Witchcraft: A History in Thirteen Trials](https://www.simonandschuster.com/books/Witchcraft/Marion-Gibson/9781668002438), Marion Gibson
- [Land Justice: Re-imagining Land, Food, and the Commons](https://archive.foodfirst.org/land-justice-re-imagining-land-food-and-the-commons/), Various
- [A Distant Mirror: The Calamitous 14th Century](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.ca/books/180834/a-distant-mirror-by-barbara-w-tuchman/9780345349576), Barbara W. Tuchman
- [How to Change Everything](https://naomiklein.org/how-to-change-everything/), Naomi Klein
  
  - I read this one to see if it might be a suitable recommendation for younger
    folks. Seems that way. Someone who has spent any significant amount of time
    reading about climate and geopolitics may not find anything new here,
    though.

- [An African History of Africa](https://www.harpercollins.ca/9780063335417/an-african-history-of-africa/), Zeinab Badawi

  - A very useful book for those (i.e. most) of us who've learned very little
    about Africa other than something focused on ancient Egyptian civilizations
    as part of high school history classes.

- [Zegaajimo](https://kegedonce.com/books/zegaajimo-indigenous-horror-fiction/), Various

  - Another good collection of Indigenous horror stories. Surprisingly, I found
    the story from Waubgeshig Rice (whose *Moon of the Crusted Snow* and *Moon
    of the Turning Leaves* I loved) underwhelming.

- [After the People Lights Have Gone Off](https://www.mcnallyrobinson.com/9781504096317/stephen-graham-jones/after-the-people-lights-have-gone-off), Stephen Graham Jones
- [One Day, Everyone Will Have Always Been Against This](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.ca/books/777485/one-day-everyone-will-have-always-been-against-this-by-omar-el-akkad/9780771021787), Omar El Akkad

  - A very timely, practical example of necropolitics at work.

- [Multispecies Cities: Solarpunk Urban Futures](https://atomicbooks.com/products/multispecies-cities-solarpunk-urban-futures), Various
- [The Buffalo Hunter Hunter](https://www.simonandschuster.ca/books/The-Buffalo-Hunter-Hunter/Stephen-Graham-Jones/9781668095485), Stephen Graham Jones

  - One of my favourite fiction reads of the year.

- [Necropolitics](https://www.dukeupress.edu/necropolitics), Achille Mbembe

  - Second time reading. Seems like it'll become a yearly look.

- [If I Must Die](https://orbooks.com/catalog/if-i-must-die/), Refaat Alareer
- [Caliban and the Witch](https://www.penguin.co.uk/books/321857/caliban-and-the-witch-by-federici-silvia/9780241532539), Silvia Federici
- [At the Trough: The Rise and Rise of Canada's Corporate Welfare Bums](https://57a2c8.myshopify.com/products/at-the-trough-the-rise-and-rise-of-canadas-corporate-welfare-bums), Laurent Carbonneau
- [Tiger Lung](https://www.darkhorse.com/products/25-911/tiger-lung-hc/), Simon Roy, Jason Wordie

  - A collection of prehistory fantasy comics. Cool concepts, but a little disjointed.

- [Buddhism: A Very Short Introduction](https://academic.oup.com/book/854), Damien Keown
- [Confucianism: A Very Short Introduction](https://academic.oup.com/book/963), Daniel K. Gardner
- [The Silmarillion](https://www.mcnallyrobinson.com/9780008537890/j-r-r-tolkien/the-silmarillion), J.R.R. Tolkien
  
  - Tolkien's works have shaped my reading and media interest for a very long
    time, so I decided to finally give this a go. While it's nice to have some
    idea of the broader mythology he had in mind, this was a brutal and boring
    slog of a read. I can't say I'll ever do so again.

- [The Wretched of the Earth](https://www.maktaba.online/en/the-wretched-of-the-earth.html), Frantz Fanon

  - I'm surprised I hadn't read this one earlier. Challenging, but it really is
    an important book.

- [The Ultimate Hidden Truth of the World](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.ca/books/722432/the-ultimate-hidden-truth-of-the-world-by-david-graeber-edited-by-nika-dubrovsky-foreword-by-rebecca-solnit/9780771004933), David Graeber

  - Posthumous release of essays and speeches by Graber. If anyone wants to get
    a feel for his thinking, this seems like a good place to start. For certain
    topics (i.e. "bullshit jobs", which is also a book), I think the essay is
    actually sufficient.

- [Never Whistle at Night: An Indigenous Dark Fiction Anthology](www.penguinrandomhouse.ca/books/746751/never-whistle-at-night-an-indigenous-dark-fiction-anthology-by-shane-hawk-and-theodore-van-alst-jr-editors/9781039003798), Various

  - This was amazing. Give it a read.

- [Black Sun](https://birchbarkbooks.com/products/black-sun), Rebecca Roanhorse
- [Hinduism: A Very Short Introduction](https://academic.oup.com/book/901), Kim Knott
- [Between Doorways: Explorations Into Liminal Space](https://www.saltheartpress.com/between-doorways), Various

  - Another short story collection, this time about "neverwheres" and in-between
    places. There are some really creepy ones in here.

- [Becoming Kin: An Indigenous Call to Unforgetting the Past and Reimagining Our Future](https://www.broadleafbooks.com/store/productgroup/3587/Becoming-Kin), Patty Krawec

  - One of my favourites.

- [Chaos: Making a New Science](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.ca/books/321477/chaos-by-james-gleick/9780143113454), James Gleick

  - This book blew my mind when I first read it in ~2009, and it helped inspire
    me to pursue a degree in physics. However, I didn't find any of that magic
    while re-reading it, and I don't think I'll read it again. Funny how that
    works.

- [Howls From the Dark Ages](https://howlsociety.com/howls-from-the-dark-ages/), Various

  - The first of many forays into short story collections of the year.

- [The Art of Not Being Governed](https://yalebooks.yale.edu/book/9780300169171/the-art-of-not-being-governed/), James C. Scott

  - Interesting overview, and it meshes well with writing from Graeber/Wengrow
    and others. I found it to be a little repetitive, though - while I
    understand this isn't my area of expertise and a lot of details given might
    be useful to similar researchers, I felt that it could've been half as long.

- [The Abrahamic Religions: A Very Short Introduction](https://academic.oup.com/book/28386), Charles L. Cohen
- [The Blacktongue Thief](https://us.macmillan.com/books/9781250799975/theblacktonguethief/), Christopher Buehlman

  - A good example of very concise worldbuilding, combined with some novel takes
    on common fantasy tropes. I hear the audiobook is also good.

- [The North-West is Our Mother](https://www.harpercollins.ca/9781443450126/the-north-west-is-our-mother/), Jean Teillet
- [The Lost World](https://michaelcrichton.com/works/the-lost-world/), Michael Crichton

  - The movie *Jurassic Park* is possibly my all-time favourite, and I love both
    books. Reading this again recently reminded me of how much more entertaining
    the second book is than the corresponding movie.

- [African Religions: A Very Short Introduction](https://academic.oup.com/book/465), Jacob K. Olupona

  - One of several religion-oriented AVSI books I had planned to read in 2025.
